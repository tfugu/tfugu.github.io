Colors Vocab

“All colors are the friends of their neighbors and the lovers of their opposites.” - Marc Chagall

Considering I can barely keep up with what’s written on the side of crayons, let’s stick with the most basic and most useful colors. Now, as I mentioned on the last page, colors can get pretty weird and complicated. There are い adjective colors, there are な adjective colors, and there are noun versions of all colors. For the vocab list, you are learning some of them as い adjectives, with the rest being learned as nouns. You’ll learn the rules behind when to use each color as an adjective or a “noun,” but for now, if you learn these words in this way you’ll come out ahead when the grammar part of the lesson starts. So, don’t worry about that too much – right now, your job is to learn the vocab. Then, we’ll use that knowledge to learn about the grammar.

Color かな Meaning
色 いろ Color
青い あおい Blue
赤い あかい Red
黄色い きいろい Yellow
黒い くろい Black
白い しろい White
ピンク ピンク Pink
紫 むらさき Purple
緑 みどり Green
オレンジ色 おれんじいろ Orange
茶色 ちゃいろ Brown
灰色 はいいろ Gray
金 きん Gold
銀 ぎん Silver
真っ黒 まっくろ Pure Black
真っ赤 まっか Pure Red
真っ白 まっしろ Pure White

There aren’t too many words here – as usual, try to cram a few of these abstract words before starting a study spurt, that way when they show up in the list, they won’t be so abstract anymore!

Color Words on Anki

This is one of the first Anki lists on TextFugu where you can generate your own lists using your own templates. Built in (and ready to go) is what you need for this lesson, but if you want to use kanji instead of kana, you can create your own templates and generate more cards to study. This is optional and “advanced” but I just wanted to let you know that customization is available!

When you’ve gotten part way through the list, feel free to take a break to read the next page or two. Make sure you get back to your list and review / learn some new words soon, though, as you’ll need them in a little bit.
